# Campaign schedule — the slot we're trying to hold

From: MSAT / program management

The pressure behind the go/no-go, stated plainly: we are holding a manufacturing campaign slot,
and the batch release deadline that follows it is what the downstream clinical schedule is built
on. Slip the slot and the next opening is a full quarter out.

The bench team has the capacity to run it now. They do not have the capacity to run it now *and*
re-validate the potency assay *and* re-test the master cell bank inside the same window — that's
the real constraint, not enthusiasm.

So the decision isn't "are we excited," it's "do we spend the slot on a run that may not survive
release, or hold it and take the schedule risk." I'd rather miss a slot than miss it twice.
