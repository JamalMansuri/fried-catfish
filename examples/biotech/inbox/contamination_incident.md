# Incident — mycoplasma signal near the master cell bank

From: QC microbiology

Flagging this before the go/no-go. A routine screen threw a low-level mycoplasma signal on a
working bank vial two passages downstream of the master cell bank. The repeat came back clean, so
this may be a sampling artifact — but a single positive on a sterility-adjacent test is not
something we get to wave off.

Bioburden and endotoxin on the actual production cells are within limits. The open question is
whether the master cell bank itself is implicated, because if it is, every downstream batch
inherits the risk and that is a patient-safety issue, not a paperwork one.

We need a clean re-test of the master cell bank before anyone signs a release.
