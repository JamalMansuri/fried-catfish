# Memo — what a GxP run commits us to

From: Regulatory affairs

Reading the room before the council romanticizes "we're basically ready." Moving to a GxP run is
not a bench decision; it is a GMP commitment the FDA can audit line by line.

- The release potency assay must be **validated**, not just qualified — and the validation report
  has to be signed before, not after, the batch.
- Equipment used in the run needs current IQ/OQ/PQ and in-date calibration records. One lapsed
  calibration certificate and the whole batch record is questionable.
- Every deviation gets written up with an audit trail under 21 CFR Part 11. A deviation handled
  informally at the bench becomes a finding at inspection.

None of this says no. It says the gate is documentation that holds up under GMP, and we are not
there on the assay validation yet.
